# Session Guide -- For Facilitators

You are running a 30-minute dojo session for a group of engineers. Your job is to keep the energy up, the clock moving, and the focus on practice over lecture.

## Before the Session

- Have your screen ready to share with a visible timer (use any browser timer or phone)
- Have the README open so attendees can see the stages and links
- Be ready to show a quick gallery of tool options during Suit Up

## The Flow

### Stage 1 -- Suit Up (3 minutes)

Put a timer on screen. Show the tool options from the [Suit Up](./suit-up/) guide.

Say something like:

> "Welcome to the dojo. For the next 30 minutes we are going to practice together. Step one -- pick your weapon. Choose one AI tool you want to get better at or one you have never tried. If you need to download something, do it now. If you already have your tool, open it up."

Show these categories on screen:
- **CLI tools**: Claude Code, Gemini CLI, Copilot CLI, Codex CLI, OpenCode, Aider
- **IDE tools**: VS Code + Copilot, Cursor, Windsurf
- **Desktop apps**: Claude Desktop, ChatGPT, Gemini, Codex

Do not explain every tool. The point is to choose fast and move.

### Stage 2 -- Basic Forms (7 minutes)

Put a timer on screen. Show the [Basic Forms](./basic-forms/) checklist.

Say something like:

> "Now we are going to practice our basic forms. These are the fundamental moves you should be able to do without thinking. Your first move -- ask your tool how it works. Every good tool has a built-in guide. Find it. Then work through the checklist."

Let people work individually. Walk around (or monitor chat) to help anyone stuck.

### Stage 3 -- Circle Up (5 minutes)

No timer needed. Open it up.

> "Pause. Let us circle up. What surprised you? What was harder than expected? What did you discover?"

Call on 2-3 people. Keep it quick. This is not a lecture -- it is a pulse check.

### Stage 4 -- Choose Your Path (10 minutes)

Put a timer on screen. Show the [paths list](./paths/).

> "Now pick one path. Any topic that interests you. You have 10 minutes to explore it. If you finish early, pick another. Go."

This is the quiet, focused part. Let people work.

### Stage 5 -- Bow Out (5 minutes)

> "Time. Let us bow out. What did you try? What clicked? What are you going to practice next?"

Call on 2-3 people. Then close:

> "The dojo is always open. The kata repo has everything we covered today plus more paths you can explore solo. The only way to get better is to practice. See you next time."

Share the repo link.

## Tips

- **Do not lecture.** The whole point is hands-on practice. Your job is to set the timer and get out of the way.
- **Mixed levels are fine.** Advanced engineers will pick harder paths. Beginners will spend more time on basics. That is by design.
- **Secure repo concerns.** Some engineers may not be able to use AI tools on their work code. Remind them they can practice on any open source repo, a personal project, or even just explore the tool's own docs.
- **Keep the dojo metaphor light.** It is a framing device, not a bit. Do not force it.
- **Run it again.** This format works as a recurring practice session. Different path each time.
